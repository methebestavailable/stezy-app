1. Getting Started

We are glad to have you on board. To help you get started, we’ve prepared this series of guides for you. In this first guide, we’ll discuss an overview of what Stezy is and what you can do with it.

What is Stezy?

Stezy is a "Do it yourself Blockchain Platform", which helps companies, integrate and deploy blockchain solutions with ease by utilizing DIY network and smart contract builder on a fully automated & secured infrastructure.

What can you do with Stezy?

There are two key components which Stezy platform deals with . These are :

  • A blockchain network also commonly known consortium and
  • A blockchain code also commonly known as smart contract

By using Stezy platform, organizations can save time, cost and effort to setup blockchain networks, invite other participants to their network and deploy smart contracts.

Who uses Stezy?

The short answer is anyone with the intent to move towards decentralization and want to build greater level of trust and transparency into their existing system. In fact, Stezy can be used by:

  • Small & large businesses who wish to bring the blockchain advantage in their product and that too on a budget.
  • Small & large service companies who wish to serve their customers with blockchain solutions without requiring to hire costly and not so easily available blockchain developers and architects.
  • Developers & Freelancers who want to quickly get their hands dirty with blockchain technology and use them in their projects or just for fun.
How different is Stezy solution from other BaaS solutions?

Stezy is the only solution of its kind which provides most flexibility to the organisations in terms of choice of consensus protocols, frameworks and deployment regions.

Moreover, Stezy provides a smart contract builder which takes away the efforts of writing programs in solidity and manually deploying it on the blockchain node you own.

2. A Quick Introduction to Blockchain | Blockchain Key Concepts

Much has been written to explain what a blockchain is. For those readers who are not sure what a blockchain is, we give a quick explanation here. If you are already familiar with blockchain, you can skip to the next section.

What is Blockchain ?

A blockchain is a shared distributed ledger. We refer to entities that participate in a blockchain as blockchain participants. It is possible for all blockchain participants to have their own copy of the ledger.

How is blockchain different from traditional ledgers and databases ?

Like any ledger, a blockchain can be used to keep track of almost anything such as money, shipments, etc. Unlike other kinds of ledgers, before a transaction can be recorded in a blockchain, its participants must agree that the transaction is valid. Participants automatically achieve consensus on the validity of transactions through their automated proxies. Something else that makes a blockchain database different than other kinds of ledgers is that once a transaction is stored in a blockchain, it cannot be changed or deleted.

Knowing that information on a blockchain represents an unaltered record of things that participants agreed were true makes a blockchain a trusted source of information. This makes blockchain technology a way of allowing parties who have conflicting interests to trust each other.

For example, there are a few blockchains* being used to track ownership of shares in corporations. The corporations and their shareholders have different interests, but they are all able to trust ownership records on the blockchain database

What is Smart Contract ?

Another feature of blockchain is smart contracts. A smart contract is a piece of code that is run when a transaction is received by a blockchain. Smart contracts can do such things as validate a transaction, trigger a payment or decide that an additional transaction should be put on the blockchain.

What is Node ?

Nodes are basically replicas of ledgers which people rely on to keep track of cryptocurrency transactions that have occurred on the blockchain. Anyone who wishes to can create a node and doing so, will have their own copy of the full blockchain transaction history

What is Consensus ?

In the blockchain context, consensus is the mechanism where the nodes, participating in the formation of the blockchain, act in concert and have a common opinion and way of cooperation. In addition, this is the way to reach agreement on the correctness of the block — confirmation that the content of the block is not falsified, is not distorted and is approved by all the nodes of the blockchain.

Different blockchain frameworks use various ways to achieve consistency and confirmation between nodes — consensus algorithms/protocols.

For example, Ethereum(geth) uses the PoA algorithm (Proof of Authority). PoA involves a set of nodes designated as trusted “validators” taking turns validating(signing) and disseminating blocks to the network.The main characteristics of a PoA network are a low requirement of computational power, no requirement of communication between nodes to reach consensus, and continuity of the network is independent of the number of the available genuine nodes since they are pre-approved and verifiably trustable through cross verification in the public domain.

Quorum on the other hand offers two options - RAFT & IBFT. The defining features of RAFT algo are Election Safety (only one leader can be elected in a given term) and Leader Append-Only (a leader never overwrites or deletes entries in the log; it only appends new entries). Raft is intended for use in a relatively small cluster of servers of around five or less.

IBFT, is a byzantine fault tolerant protocol that follows the PBFT implementation and is based on a commitment consensus where each node waits until 2F + 1 commits from different validators with the same result before inserting the block into the blockchain. Additionally, IBFT offers further protections through the collection of signatures from the block proposer and voting validator nodes.

3. Product Guide

You can elect for one of two routes to perform any operation on the Stezy platform from building your first consortium, deploying smart contracts to setup the complete underlying environment. The first approach relies solely on the user interface, while the second option allows you to exercise the Stezy fully RESTful API to send privileged calls to the backend server. Regardless of your selection, you must visit the Stezy dashboard and create an account prior to continuing.

In this article we will guide you with the approach of setting things up using user interface. For guide on setting things up using RESTful APIs, refer the API 101 article.

Create your Network
  • Open the Create Blockchain Dialog
  • Provide your network, a suitable name and description on the purpose of blockchain
  • Select the blockchain Framework and the underlying Consensus Protocol.
  • Select the Cloud Provider where you wish to deploy your blockchain nodes.
  • Enter the Node name & description
  • Select the Geo and the Region where you want to host the blockchain node.
  • Enter the Payment Information and Deploy your blockchain.
  • Wait for few minutes till blockchain status changes to Running
Manage your Network

Open the “View & Manage” Dialog from a particular blockchain tab. It takes you to that particular blockchain console where you can view and manage various blockchain components.

  • Genesis File : The genesis block is the start of the blockchain, and the genesis.json is the file that defines it. It is like the “settings” for your blockchain. For example, the chain configuration, level of difficulty to mine blocks, etc. Download it to check the unique chain configuration and view list of user accounts. It’s a read only operation.
  • Blockchain Explorer : The blockchain explorer showcases the blockchain network statistics and helps the blockchain participants track the transactions done on the blockchain.
  • Create Node : Create a new node for the blockchain. The node is owned by the organisation which initiates its creation.
    • Provide your new node, a suitable name and description on the purpose of the node.
    • Select the Geo and the Region where you want to host the blockchain node.
    • Wait for the status of this new node to change to Running
  • Invite Organisation : Create an invitation for the blockchain network.

    Inviting a new organisation to be part of existing blockchain network is an important part in decentralisation. There are two scenarios how an organisation can be invited to be part of the blockchain network :

    • New organisation is registered on Stezy :
      • Specify the organisationId (Where to find organisationId) of the organisation you want to invite.
      • Specify the details of the node invited organisation will own.
      • Send an inviite
    • New organisation not registered on Stezy.
      • New organisation need to be invited through email.
      • Once they register on Stezy platform, follow the instructions mentioned in previous scenario
  • Manage Nodes

    The organisation can view and manage the blockchain nodes owned by it and have the read only access to other blockchain nodes owned by other organisations.

    • Connect to your Node
    • Cancel Node Subscription
Create Smart Contract

Smart Contract provides the means through which participants submit transactions on the blockchain to store data. To build a new smart contract :

  • Open the Create Smart Contract Dialog
  • Provide your smart contract, a suitable name
  • Build your smart contract through simple drag and drop
  • Once the field names and types are finalised, initiate the smart contract Deployment
  • Select the blockchain and blockchain node on which this smart contract should be deployed.
  • Wait for few seconds till you get the confirmation.
  • Your smart contract will be replicated automatically to all the participating nodes in the blockchain.
Manage your Smart Contract
  • Go to Application : Create a new data entry into the blockchain
    • Enter the data you want to put on the blockchain.
    • Select the node through which this transaction should be submitted.
    • Submit the transaction and wait for few seconds till you get the confirmation and the Transaction Hash.
  • View Reports : Helps the blockchain participants track the transactions done through a particular smart contract.
    • Transaction Hash : Transaction hash (txid) is an identifier used to uniquely identify a particular transaction.
    • Record Id : Unique identifier used by the smart contract while querying a particular record from the blockchain.
    • View Receipt : Gives information about the transaction, concerning its execution.
      • Transaction Index : Index of the transaction in the block.
      • Block Hash : Hash of the block where this transaction is stored.
      • Block Number : Number/Height of the block where this transaction is stored.
      • Gas Used : The amount of gas used by this specific transaction alone.
      • Cumulative Gas Used :The total amount of gas used when this transaction was executed in the block. In other words it is gas used by the transaction plus gas used by the transactions executed before the current one in the same block.

5. Billing

Billing is at the organization level while preserving visibility into usage in each project. Stezy charges the organisations only for the blockchain nodes that are provisioned by them. Stezy charges monthly for your blockchain nodes. If organisations decide to cancel their ongoing node subscription, they will continue to use all the services till their plan’s current expiry date is reached and their card won’t be charged for any upcoming months till they plan to start their subscription again.

There is no extra cost involved for using any of the smart contract builder services.

For all the plans, organisations have access to our complete API library for no extra cost involved.

Stezy currently is offering three plans : Starter, Standard and Enterprise. Organisations can choose the plan that best meet their needs.

6. Other FAQs

Q : How do I know which blockchain framework or protocol I should select?

A : The selection of blockchain framework and protocol is based on the use case. Please refer our product guide to gain some insight about different blockchain protocols Stezy supports. If still in doubt , write to Stezy support team to get help.

Q : What AWS Regions does Stezy support?

A : Stezy supports all AWS regions other than those in China and US GovCloud. For more information, see Amazon Web Services.

Q : What cloud providers does Stezy support?

A : Currently only AWS is enabled. We are working on providing you the flexibility to opt for multiple cloud. Please keep watching our News & Updates section for any feature updates.

Q : What is the solution, if we want to deploy our nodes in our in house cloud or the cloud provider not available on Stezy?
